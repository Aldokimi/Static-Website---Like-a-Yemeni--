History of Music in Yemen:

Some Historians said that the first songs in Yemen appeared on the 5th or the 6th century by a king called ( Als Ben Zayd Dhe Gadn ) in Arabic "علس بن زيد بن ذي جدن"and he was on of the Himyarite Kingdom (110 BCE – 525 CE) which is one of the oldest Kingdoms (click here for more informations). So he has been called Dhe Gadn because he had a very nice voice also Dhe Gadn means in Arabic "the beautiful voice". Historians also says that he was the first one who sings about Yemen. And the author of "the history of the Arabic music" Theopharastos (371 BCE – 287 BCE) said that there were 4 kingdoms in the Gulf of Aqaba ( this was the old name of the South Arabia that time) and those 4 kingdoms was " Saba" , " Hadramout" , "Qataban", and "Mamlaa" and Eratosthenes(276 BCE – 194 BCE) said there were in the Gulf of Aqaba "Maeen" and its capital is "Qaranaw", "Saba" and its capital is "Mareb", "Qataban" and its capital is "Tamna", and "Hadramout" and its capital is "Shabwah" so form this we infer that there was civilization and if there was any civilization there should appear with it many different kind of art! This was in the old civilization but for the new civilization like before 1000 Years or more there were a lot of music and singing arts including poetry on of the most famous Yemeni poets is Emro Alqaeis " امرؤ القيس"he was so great to the extent that we are studying his art till now in Yemen. Old songs :

The new song in Yemen preserved its old heritage character and added a new update commensurate with the times. With the proliferation of social media, a lot of kinds of songs appeared some of them is influenced by western culture but most of them are originally Arabic and Yemeni. There are strikingly many styles of singing in Yemen: there are Sanaaani, al-Hadrami, Adani, al-Lahji, alYafei, al-Thami, al-Taizi, and others. This is due to the isolation between the environments of a single society. Isolation is due to the geographical topography of mountains, rivers, deserts, and other isolation due to different social conditions within a single language community. Famous singers:

1- Fouad Al-Kipsi " فؤاد الكبسي" :he is among the most popular singers and musicians in Yemen. Most of his music are of ancient scholarly and oral traditions of Yemen. He is also son of the famous Yemeni poet, Abdulla Hashim Al-Kibsi. He was born in 1961 in Sanaa. He learned music from his father and he was influenced by famous Yemeni singers like Al-Sunaidar, Al-Anisi, and Al-Harithi. He studied commerce in Sanaa university and he also Studied Quranic studies and Arabic syntax and morphology.

2- Faisel Alawi "فيصل علوي1949 – 2010": he is a Yemeni singer and artist is considered a pioneer of the Lahji song. He was famous for playing the oud and became famous in the Arabian Peninsula and the Gulf countries. The artist, Faisal Alawi, was famous for playing the oud with amazing professionalism, and the artist Faysal stated, "I developed the oud that I play on to 14 strings and added 16 strings to it, and he is able to add strings to the oud to be the father of 18 strings." Those interested in the singing art movement in Yemen consider Faisal Alawi better than playing the oud, and even praise his method known as "Al-Faisaliah" and Faisal's Hijaziyat to play this instrument, which is present with him in all his songs and he excelled in playing it.

3- Hamoud Al-Sema" حمود السمه": he is a Yemeni singer, composer, and poet. He is the son of the late Yemeni artist Ali Abdullah Al-Samah. He has many songs that were famous locally and in the Arab world and he is one of the most famous Yemeni singers right now. Most of the Yemenis listen to his songs almost daily and is so respectful in the Yemeni and the Arabic world.
